Healthcare AI Readiness Assessment

Self-Assessment Tool for Hospital AI Implementation Readiness

Use this assessment to determine your hospital or health network's readiness to deploy AI-powered care prediction systems, including NDN Care Predict. Score each item honestly — results guide your implementation roadmap, not a pass/fail gate.

Compliance Note: Healthcare AI deployments must meet HIPAA, HL7/FHIR, and applicable state regulations. Items marked with compliance requirements are non-negotiable prerequisites.

1 Data Governance & Compliance

  • HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) process in place HIPAA Required
    Formal process to execute BAAs with AI vendors before data sharing
  • Patient data de-identification capability HIPAA Required
    Ability to de-identify PHI for model training per Safe Harbor or Expert Determination methods
  • Data governance committee or officer exists
    Designated owner for healthcare data policies and AI data use approvals
  • Audit logging for data access
    Full audit trails on who accessed patient data and when

2 EHR & Interoperability

  • EHR system supports HL7/FHIR APIs Integration
    Epic, Cerner, Meditech, or other FHIR R4-compatible system
  • Historical admission/discharge/transfer (ADT) data accessible
    Minimum 3 years of patient encounter data for model training
  • Structured clinical data available
    Lab results, vitals, diagnoses (ICD-10), medications in structured format
  • IT team can expose sandbox API environment
    Safe test environment for integration validation without touching live data

3 Clinical & Operational Readiness

  • Clinical champion identified
    CMO, CNO, or department head committed to AI adoption
  • Readmission is a tracked quality metric
    Hospital tracks 30-day all-cause or condition-specific readmission rates
  • Care coordination team in place
    Discharge planning nurses or case managers who will act on AI alerts
  • Workflow integration plan defined
    Clear plan for how AI risk scores surface in EHR or care team dashboards

4 Technology & Infrastructure

  • Cloud or on-prem compute environment available
    GCP, AWS, Azure — or on-prem servers sized for ML workloads
  • Network security meets healthcare standards
    Encrypted data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Vendor security review process established
    IT and legal can conduct security assessments for AI software vendors
